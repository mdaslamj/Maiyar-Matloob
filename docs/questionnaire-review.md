## Issue 1 — Q20 and Q48: Control of Speech

### Q20

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Question number** | Q20 (`M020`) |
| **Section** | اسلامی فکر و طرزِ زندگی |
| **Category** | اسلامی شخصیت |
| **Current wording** | کیا میری زندگی کے تمام شعبوں میں اسلامی شخصیت اور کردار نمایاں ہے؟ |
| **Reviewer comment** | Q20 and Q48 appear to ask about similar behaviour (control of speech). |
| **Initial analysis** | Q20 is a broad, integrative question about whether Islamic character is visible across *all* domains of life — worship, conduct, dealings, and lifestyle. It does not mention speech, tongue, or gheebah explicitly. However, because “کردار” and “اخلاق” are inclusive terms, a respondent may mentally include speech control when answering. Q48 is narrow and explicit about tongue protection (جھوٹ، غیبت، چغلی، بدزبانی، فضول گفتگو). The more direct speech-related overlap in the instrument is arguably **Q34** (امانت و دیانت — جھوٹ، دھوکہ، خیانت، وعدہ خلافی), which shares the “جھوٹ” dimension with Q48 but frames it under trustworthiness rather than tongue discipline. Q20 ↔ Q48 overlap is therefore **conceptual and scope-based**, not textual duplication. |
| **Recommendation** | **Keep** |
| **Status** | Pending Review |

### Q48

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Question number** | Q48 (`M048`) |
| **Section** | تنظیمی زندگی و استقامت |
| **Category** | زبان کی حفاظت |
| **Current wording** | کیا میں اپنی زبان کو جھوٹ، غیبت، چغلی، بدزبانی اور فضول گفتگو سے محفوظ رکھتا ہوں / رکھتی ہوں؟ |
| **Reviewer comment** | Q20 and Q48 appear to ask about similar behaviour (control of speech). |
| **Initial analysis** | Q48 has a distinct category (زبان کی حفاظت) and a specific behavioural checklist tied to speech ethics. Q20 measures holistic Islamic personality, not speech alone. If a respondent scores high on Q20 because they perceive good character generally, they may also score high on Q48 — producing correlated but not redundant measurement. Clarification in Q20’s explanation (that it is not a substitute for domain-specific items like Q48) could reduce perceived duplication without merging questions. Cross-reference: Q34 also tests avoidance of جھوٹ; domain review should consider whether Q34 and Q48 boundaries are sufficiently distinct (deception/trust vs. comprehensive tongue protection). |
| **Recommendation** | **Keep** |
| **Status** | Pending Review |

## Issue 2 — Q47: Overlap with Nearby Questions

### Q47

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Question number** | Q47 (`M047`) |
| **Section** | تنظیمی زندگی و استقامت |
| **Category** | دین کو ترجیح |
| **Current wording** | کیا میں ذاتی مفاد، خواہش اور سہولت پر دین کو مقدم رکھتا ہوں / رکھتی ہوں؟ |
| **Reviewer comment** | Q47 may overlap conceptually with nearby questions. |
| **Initial analysis** | Q47 sits in the closing “تنظیمی زندگی و استقامت” block alongside: **Q46** (انفاق — financial sacrifice for deen), **Q48** (زبان کی حفاظت), and **Q49** (وفاداری و ذمہ داری). Q46 and Q48 are behaviourally distinct. Q47’s unique angle is *conflict resolution*: when personal interest, desire, or convenience conflicts with deen, which side wins? Broader thematic neighbours earlier in the instrument include **Q43** (اقامتِ دین as life responsibility), **Q44** (aligning all activities with Allah’s pleasure and deen’s purpose), and **Q45** (continuous progress in iman and da‘wah). **Q47 vs Q44** is the closest pair: Q44 asks about aligning daily activities with divine purpose; Q47 asks about prioritizing deen when there is explicit tension with self-interest. A respondent could answer both similarly if they interpret Q44 broadly. Distinction is real but subtle — may benefit from explanation refinement rather than structural change. |
| **Recommendation** | **Keep** |
| **Status** | Pending Review |

| **Initial analysis** | Q50 is intentionally **synthetic and closing** — a comprehensive muhāsaba of life’s ultimate purpose.
